Air Pollutants Emissions among the World (1922 ~ 2022)

From 1922 to 2022, global air pollutant emissions reflect the effects of industrialization, urbanization, and environmental policies. Initially, pollutants like NOx, SO₂, and CO rose with industrial growth, particularly in developed countries. However, stricter regulations later reduced emissions, especially in these regions. Meanwhile, developing areas experienced rising emissions due to urban and industrial expansion. This timeline shows both progress in air quality and ongoing pollution challenges.

Pollutants Emissions per Population by Year

Carbon monoxide is the leading air pollutant globally, with high emissions in nearly every country. It mainly results from vehicle exhaust, industrial activities, and fossil fuel burning. The widespread prevalence shows that carbon monoxide pollution is a global concern, requiring coordinated efforts to reduce its impact on air quality and public health.

Black carbon is one of the least emitted air pollutants globally, with lower emissions across most countries. Primarily produced by incomplete combustion of biomass and fossil fuels, its localized sources contribute to its smaller global footprint. Variations in industrial practices and energy use also influence its lower emission levels. This highlights the need for targeted strategies to address the widespread issue of carbon monoxide while noting the lesser challenge posed by black carbon emissions.

Pollutants Emissions in Different Continents

Carbon Monoxide (CO) is the most emitted pollutant globally, with consistently high levels across all regions, while Black Carbon (BC) shows negligible emissions worldwide. Ammonia (NH₃) emissions vary significantly by region, with North America having the lowest levels from 1922 to 2022, compared to higher emissions in Asia and Europe. In the 19th century, air pollution was primarily driven by emissions from North America and Europe due to the Industrial Revolution. In the early 20th century, Asia saw a significant rise in emissions as industrialization expanded in the region, reflecting its growing role in global industrial production and its impact on air quality.

Pollutants Emission in Malaysia

In the 1980s, Malaysia's rapid industrialization and economic transformation led to a sharp rise in air pollutants, particularly carbon monoxide (CO). This increase reflects growing industrial activity, energy consumption, and transportation needs, driven by the country's shift to a manufacturing-based economy, resulting in higher pollution from fossil fuels and industrial processes.
